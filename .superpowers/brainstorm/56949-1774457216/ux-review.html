UI/UX Review: Fitness Device Lab

An expert critique based on Apple HIG and athletic performance requirements.

STRENGTHS

  • Glanceable Information: The high-contrast data fields in the Workout Player are excellent for mid-effort checks.
  • Clear Hierarchy: The use of TabView for primary navigation provides a standard, easy-to-learn structure.
  • Contextual Feedback: Color-coding for workout zones is a great way to communicate intensity without text.

AREAS FOR IMPROVEMENT

  • Interaction Safety: Some buttons are too small for "sweaty hands" or high-intensity moments.
  • Information Density: The Devices tab can feel cluttered when multiple sensors are active.
  • Navigation Transitions: Moving between Library and Player could be more fluid to reduce friction before a ride.

Deep Dive: Interaction Safety

During a hard interval (Z4+), fine motor skills degrade. The current 44x44pt touch targets are the minimum recommended by HIG, but for an athletic app, we should aim for 60x60pt or larger for critical mid-workout actions like "Lap" or "Pause".
